The pipeline bubble, and how block-wise local training removes it

Each square is one rack in one time step. Forward and backward shown separately.
forward passbackward passidle (bubble)
Pipeline parallelism (left): rack 0 finishes its forwards early, then sits idle for six steps waiting for gradients to come back from the deeper racks before it can run its own backward. That middle idle band is the bubble, and it grows with the number of racks. BLT (right): each rack works its own stream of microbatches, alternating forward and backward one pass per step at the same rate as the left, but with its own local loss it never waits on a global backward. After a one-step-per-depth fill it simply never idles.
