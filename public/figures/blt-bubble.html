The pipeline bubble, and how block-wise local training removes it

Each square is one rack in one time step. Forward and backward shown separately.
forward passbackward passidle (bubble)
Pipeline parallelism (left): rack 0 finishes its forwards early, then sits idle for six steps waiting for gradients to come back from the deeper racks before it can run its own backward. That middle idle band is the bubble, and it grows with the number of racks. BLT (right): each rack runs its own forward and backward every step (split squares), so after a one-step-per-depth fill nobody waits on a global backward. No drain, no bubble.
