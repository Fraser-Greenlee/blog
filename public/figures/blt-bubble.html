The pipeline bubble, and how block-wise local training removes it

Each square is one rack doing work in one time step. Grey = idle.
Pipeline parallelism (left): racks wait for the forward wavefront to arrive and for the backward pass to drain; the grey corners are wasted GPU time, and they grow with the number of racks. BLT (right): after a one-step fill, every rack runs its own forward, local loss, and backward every step. No global backward, no drain.
