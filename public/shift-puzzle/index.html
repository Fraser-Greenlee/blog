How to play

Make your board match the Target above. On bigger boards only the outlined window has to match — the rest is free space to work in.

The four edge arrows shift the whole board one step — cells that fall off one side wrap around to the other.

The four corner buttons shift the cells one step around each ring: the right corners move them clockwise (↻), the left corners anticlockwise (↺). The centre cell of an odd grid never moves.

Each board has a par — a few moves above the shortest possible solution. Reach the target within par to solve it. Taking the move that reverses your last one steps you back and gives the move back (it's highlighted), so exploring a dead end costs nothing if you undo it.

From level 8 on, pieces carry a facing (the white arrow) that must match the target too. A piece turns 90° only as it leaves a corner of its ring; sliding it along a straight edge, or into a corner, leaves it pointing the same way. (One full lap is four corners, a complete turn.) The centre piece spins in place with every ring move.

From level 12 the cells are tiles of a picture to reassemble; the turning variants also need each tile the right way up. On the hidden levels the target is a ? until you solve it.

The Candy modes are an endless match game: fill a whole row or column with matching candies to clear them, then everything falls and new candies drop in. You have a limited number of moves — each move spends one, and each cleared line earns several back, so you have to keep clearing or you run out and the run ends. The facing and along-line variants also need the candies' arrows to agree. Bad candies (dark) can't be matched and you lose if three ever line up; blanks (grey) are inert clutter. The reverse move (highlighted) takes back a move that didn't clear anything; Reset starts a fresh run.

The shapes candy modes show a coloured shape to Make (plus a Next preview so you can plan ahead). Form that shape in that colour anywhere it fits on the board and it clears, earns moves, and the next target slides in. The + axis variant also needs every cell's bar to lie the same way the target shows — all upright or all sideways. The two shapes mode shows two different-colour shapes you must form on the board at the same time to clear together. The shapes are always built from colours you actually have, so they're always possible.

Keyboard: arrows shift, Q/E ring-shift, R reset, N new board.
